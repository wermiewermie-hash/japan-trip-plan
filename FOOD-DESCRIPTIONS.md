### 🍱 Kaiseki
`key: kaiseki`
Description: Formal, traditional 12 course meal of local dishes from seasonal ingredients. Often considered the summit of Japanese Washoku. 

---

### 🍤 Tempura
`key: tempura`
Description: Very different from tempura in the States. Heavy focus on very seasonal ingredients and rare vegetables, battered and fried right before you eat them. Served in courses.

---

### 🍖 Tonkatsu
`key: tonkatsu`
Description: So much better than the dry stuff you get in the states. Often made from heritage Japanese pork, and you can specify the cuts to order. Try fried oysters too. 

---

### ☕ Old-timey cafés
`key: cafes`
Description: Kissaten — old fashioned wood-panelled cafés from the Showa era, where coffee is brewed by hand and the menu has throwback items like cream sodas and fruit parfaits. Try the coffee jelly. 

---

### 🫘 Coffee roasters
`key: roasters`
Description: Not sure how good Japanese roasters are tbh, but worth a visit!

---

### 🍸 Bars
`key: bars`
Description: Classic bars are small, intimate bars often with wood paneling and prohibition era decor. Rowdier local bars are still small, but offer trashy drinks and fun karaoke. 

---

### 🥘 Okonomiyaki
`key: okonomiyaki`
Description: A Kansai local's favorite. Kind of like a Japanese pizza. A cabbage-and-batter pancake griddled at the table and finished with brown sauce, bonito flakes, and mayonnaise.

---

### 🍲 Yuba
`key: yuba`
Description: The delicate skin lifted from simmering soy milk — a refined Kyoto specialty served fresh, rolled, or simmered.

---

### 🥬 Kyo Tsukemono
`key: tsukemono`
Description: Kyoto's celebrated pickles — vegetables cured in salt, rice bran, or sake lees, bright and sharp alongside almost every meal. Shibazuke (cucumber with Shiso and sendai zuke daikon sliced paper thin) are local favorites. 

---

### 🧊 Kyo Tofu
`key: tofu`
Description: Kyoto is known for its varieties of tofu, not just made of regular soy beans. Try chewy sesame seed tofu, vegetal edameme tofu, soft custardy egg tofu. 

---

### 🐟 Ayu
`key: ayu`
Description: Ayu is a river fish that's caught locally in the summer. Often prepared as tempura in a "Sugata age" where it looks like it's swimming on your plate. Great to enjoy beside a river in Kibune. 

---

### 🍜 Somen
`key: somen`
Description: A Japanese summer staple, fine white wheat noodles served ice-cold with a light fish broth. Nara is the birthplace of somen. 

---

### 🥢 Soba
`key: soba`
Description: Buckwheat noodles with a nutty depth, served chilled with dipping sauce or hot in broth. 

---

### 🍶 Izakaya
`key: izakaya`
Description: Japanese for "A place to stay and drink" that serves as an evening third space for most middle class people. The goal is stay a while and enjoy the company, not gorge on food and immediately leave. Some places are open all night. 

---

### 🍢 Yakitori
`key: yakitori`
Description: Beak to tail (?) skewers, where almost every part of the chicken is eaten including skin, heart and cartilage. Often enjoyed casually with friends after work with cheap drinks, and is meant to be unstuffy middle class food, not a high end delicacy. 

---

### 🍵 Tea
`key: tea`
Description: Uji, one town over from Kyoto, as well as Shizuoka (where Izu is) are traditional tea growing centers. Definitely try the cold brewed sencha in summer months. 

---

### 🍱 Ekiben
`key: ekiben`
Description: The station lunchbox — a regional bento bought on the platform and eaten on the train, every area with its own specialty.
